ITO-based OLED Device Fabrication & Optoelectronic Performance Analysis

Device Physics / OLED Fabrication & Characterization • 2026. 03. 01. ~ 2026. 06. 30. • 5 min read
Share on LinkedIn Send via Email

(Fabrication of Organic Light Emitting Diodes on ITO glass anodes, utilizing Blade Coating and analyzing optoelectronic reliability J-V-L profiles.)

1) Project Overview

  • Period: 2026. 03. 01. ~ 2026. 06. 30.
  • Type: Semiconductor Equipment & Device Reliability Analysis
  • Organization: Dept. of Electronic Biophysics, Kwangwoon University
  • Summary: Fabricating organic light-emitting devices through chemical acid pre-cleaning of ITO, blade-coating emitting layers, and executing two-step thermal baking to achieve 2nd rank overall among 8 competing batches.

2) Background & Problem Statement

  • Background: Maximizing the efficiency of optoelectronic devices requires optimizing surface energy and ensuring uniform layer thickness to lower potential barriers at the anode interface.
  • Problem Statement: While blade coating is highly material-efficient compared to spin coating, it suffers from poor film thickness uniformity, risking leakage currents, high turn-on voltages, and significant efficiency roll-off at high voltages.

3) Objectives

  • Quantitative Targets:
    • Control the turn-on voltage (Von) below 4.0 V.
    • Achieve peak luminance (Lmax) above 4,000 cd/m² and optimize performance at mid-voltage sweeps (6 V).
    • Profile and maximize EQE (External Quantum Efficiency) and CE (Current Efficiency) in valid ranges (> 10 cd/m²).
  • Qualitative Targets: Establish optical detection rules for ITO anodes, optimize surface wetting via acid cleaner pre-treatments, and stabilize double-pass reverse coating profiles.

4) Key Contributions (My Role)

  • Anode Edge Refraction Profiling: Identified the active ITO-coated side of the glass by inspecting edge refraction zones (purple light reflection denotes the glass side), completely avoiding electrode contact failure.
  • Acid Pre-Cleaning Control: Applied reactive acid solutions to strip contaminants and surface oxide films, successfully optimizing the substrate's wetting angle.
  • Double-Pass Reverse Blade Coating: Controlled blade-gap variables and syringe feed rates, executing a reverse-direction 2nd coat followed by 200°C thermal baking to yield high-quality emitting layers.
  • J-V-L Data Profiling: Evaluated real-time Current Density (J) and Luminance (L) across a 0V-12V sweep, generating comprehensive J-V-L curves and CIE chromaticity movement paths.

6) Implementation Pipeline

1. Substrate Fabrication & Acid Cleaning

Verified the inactive side of the ITO glass by checking the purple boundary lines. Initiated chemical cleaning to clear micro-contaminants, establishing superior surface conditions for the deposition steps.

2. Emitting Layer Coating & Crystallization

Formed a stable meniscus boundary between the blade edge and the substrate. Programmed a two-step temperature profile up to 200°C to dry solvents and accelerate film crystallization kinetics. Compensated for thickness variation with a reverse-path 2nd coating process.

3. Device Sweeping & Analytics

Evaluated the finished device by applying a 0V to 12V voltage sweep. Collected J-V-L data points, isolating valid ranges (> 10 cd/m²) to filter out detector measurement noise.

7) Key Troubleshooting Points

  • Characterizing High-Voltage Efficiency Roll-off:

    Observed a rapid 93.2% drop in luminance from 10.5 V (4,071 cd/m²) to 12 V. While current density (J) remained high at 64 mA/cm², the lack of light emission pointed directly to Exciton-Exciton Annihilation and Joule Heating-induced damage within the emitting layer, causing carriers to bypass radiative recombination.

  • Defect Root Cause Investigation (jyp Sample):

    Detected a severe leakage current (2.875 mA/cm² @ 4V) followed by a non-monotonic voltage-current drop in a competitor's device. Diagnosed this as an electrical short path triggered by micro-cracks or dust contamination during cutting/cleaning, proving the criticality of pre-clean steps in packaging yield.

8) Key Results & Achievements

  • Ranked 2nd Overall: Achieved 2nd place overall among 8 competing student groups based on cumulative metrics.
  • No.1 in Mid-Voltage Luminance: Logged 119.2 cd/m² @ 6V, demonstrating unmatched power-to-light conversion efficiency in typical operating windows.
  • Optimal CIE Color Saturation: Achieved CIE x = 0.5555, y = 0.4417, yielding the purest yellow emission hue among all test batches.

Gained a core understanding that minor deviations in process parameters (pre-treatment, temperature) determine the performance and yield of final electronic devices. Realized the significance of balancing peak conversion efficiency with high-voltage durability constraints.
